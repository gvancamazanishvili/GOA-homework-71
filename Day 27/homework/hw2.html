croissant

  1. butter
  2. flour
  3. sugar
  4. salt
  5. yeast
  6. milk

The Process

  1. Make easy dough from butter, flour, sugar, salt, yeast, and milk.
  2. Roll out dough into a large rectangle.
  3. Make the butter layer
  4. Enclose the butter layer inside the dough.
  5. Roll out the dough into another large rectangle, then fold it back together.
  6. Roll out the dough again, fold it back together again.
  7. Roll out the dough one more time, fold it back together.
  8. Shape the croissants.
  9. Bake!

Homemade Caramel Apples

  1. Heavy Cream
  2. Light Corn Syrup
  3. Brown Sugar
  4. Butter
  5. Salt & Vanilla Extrac

The Process

  1. Prep the apples: Wash and thoroughly dry the apples. Remove stems and insert wooden sticks firmly into the top of each apple. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Make the caramel: In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine brown sugar, corn syrup, and 1/2 cup of the heavy cream. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture begins to boil.
  3. Add remaining cream: Slowly pour in the remaining 1/2 cup cream while stirring (mixture will bubble vigorously). Continue cooking, stirring frequently, until the mixture reaches 240°F (soft ball stage) on a candy thermometer, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Finish the caramel: Remove from heat and quickly stir in butter, salt, and vanilla extract. Let cool for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened but still pourable.
  5. Dip the apples: Working quickly, tilt the pan and dip each apple into the caramel, turning to coat completely. Let excess drip off, then place on the prepared parchment pap
  6. Set: Allow caramel to cool and set for about 30 minutes before serving.

Triple Chocolate Cake

  1. All-Purpose Flour
  2. Unsweetened Natural Cocoa Powder
  3. Baking Soda & Baking Powder
  4. Salt
  5. Oil
  6. Eggs
  7. Buttermilk
  8. Vanilla Extract
  9. Hot Coffee or Hot Water

The Process

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In another bowl, beat eggs, then mix in buttermilk, hot coffee (or hot water), oil, and vanilla.
  4. Combine: Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Fold in chocolate chips. The batter will be thin - this is normal!
  5. Bake: Divide batter between prepared pans. Bake 30-35 minutes until a toothpick inserted in center comes out clean.
  6. Cool: Cool in pans 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks.
  7. ssemble: Once completely cool, frost with chocolate frosting and garnish with chocolate shavings.
