leonardo da vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was an Italian polymath—painter, sculptor, architect, scientist, and inventor—widely considered one of the greatest artists of the Renaissance. Known for masterpieces like the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, his work combined artistic brilliance with scientific observation.

last Supper

The Last Supper (1495–1498) is a monumental mural painted on the wall of the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan. It depicts the dramatic moment Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him. Leonardo broke tradition by capturing the apostles' emotional reactions in vivid detail, using innovative perspective and composition. Unlike fresco techniques, he experimented with dry paint, leading to its rapid deterioration. Despite damage and restorations, it remains one of history's most iconic religious artworks
